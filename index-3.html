FLEXIBLE VOLUNTEER OPPORTUNITIES

Not only does volunteering mean you’re making a meaningful contribution to society, and likely helping others, but it can also beef up your resume, allow you try something new that could lead to a career change, and provide expanded networking opportunities, among other things..

Those with flexible jobs often find themselves with better work-life balance, whether it’s because they’re afforded the ability to go for a run in the middle of the day, pick up the kids from school, or end the workday earlier without a daunting commute. Flexible and remote work can also give employees more room in their day to take care of non-work-related things that are important to them, like volunteering!

A flexible schedule can allow you to take a few hours off during the workday to volunteer. With this type of schedule, you can cut out an hour of your time here and there to dedicate time during normal business hours when nonprofits, schools, or organizations are operating and need assistance.
